Working with the medical device company UNYQ , we created a new type of SLS printed scoliosis brace design. UNYQ's mission is to fight against the stigma associated with wearing a medical device by infusing a sense of uniqueness in every product. We improved upon their design process, by utilizing all the opportunities additive manufacturing affords to create an appealing, breathable and lightweight solution.
Press Release: Scoliosis Brace




Typical scoliosis braces are shaped based on the patients body and unique spine curvature. The brace is typically thermoformed on a mold, making it inconvenient to add patterns and remove excess material.



Using the strap and pad locations as constraints we used topology optimization to remove excess material.



We also included a 3D printed buckle mechanism to replace the standard nylon straps, removing a major post processing step to improve manufacturing time.



Then, rather than place the pattern of the brace randomly on the surface, we did another FEA to locate areas under less stress and introduced porosity.



The final design is a process rather than a form. Every patient receives a unique brace that is optimized for his/her treatment.



The final design for one of braces was acquired by the Smithsonian Cooper Hewitt Design Museum in New York City
