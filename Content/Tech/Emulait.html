The client wanted to address the issue of nipple confusion. Babies often have trouble latching when moving back and forth between breast to bottle feeding. Emulait hired us to come up with a method for capturing the shape of the mothers breast to mitigate the physical experience of breastfeeding to bottle feeding. We organized a study of over 500 patients to validate the algorithm, and use clustering to identify 5 meaningful typologies.

We did not want to assume that nipple width was enough of differentiator between patients, so we needed an accessible, reliable and high resolution method for capturing depth. Studying various technologies available we settled on the front camera (TrueDepth Camera) available on all iPhones 11 and above.




Based on a small initial sample of approximately 10 patients, we defined a user-friendly process to gather data, and we built an algorithm using python (Open3D, Numpy, Scipy, Pandas, Matplotlib, etc.) that extracted vital measurements, key 3D points, and rebuild the nipple for manufacturing. The first part of the algorithm oriented each scan along a common axis through the optimization of a few key metrics.




After orientation, the second part of the algorithm gradually filtered out vertices based on their estimated normals until a clear separation was found between the base and tip of the edge of the nipple.





We organized a large study of patients from diverse backgrounds to start the process of building a dataset for 2D detection and fine tune the algorithm. The result was a dataset of scans, colors and 3D key points over 500 scans from patients across different backgrounds.




We used PCA analysis, outlier detection and data segmentation of the large dataset to determine five meaningful typologies and colors. Because, the final capturing process needed to be automated we also utilized the dataset to train object detection models to recognize the patient's nipple in 3D scans and 2D images.
